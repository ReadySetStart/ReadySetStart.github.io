Ready Set Start

Where everyone is an artist

Our Mission

Ready Set Start works to increase arts accessibility by bringing myriad creative expression workshops to communities that lack access to the arts. In doing this, we use the arts as a source of empowerment and personal growth, ultimately eliminating the stigma that art can only be practiced by experts and replacing it with the idea that it in fact enriches all of our lives.

Core Values

Creativity

We encourage people to use all the facets of their imagination to explore their abilities in art.

Inclusiveness

We support the idea that anyone can benefit from exposure to all forms of art.

Empowerment

We aim to use our abilities in arts to help communities.


Ready Set Start founded

We began to focus our passions for the arts by beginning to explore arts accessibility. The idea for this organization evolved from a leadership class project for the Lloyd Hall Scholars Program at the University of Michigan.

November 2014

Registered as a Student Voluntary Organization

We expanded our recruitment efforts and started to work with other talented student artists within the University of Michigan campus.

January 2015

Launched pilot program at the Bryant Community Center in Ann Arbor

We began our arts workshops at a community center for elementary school students. Working with students age 5-10, we taught workshop in visual arts, music, dance, and creative writing. We gathered positive feedback about our program and collected statistics that evidenced an increase in arts interest in the students we worked with.

January 2015

Won $5000 as a Finalist in the 2015 Optimize Social Innovation Challenge

We were selected as 1 of 5 finalists from 25 team talented teams in the University of Michigan campus wide challenge. Competing amongst other teams driven towards social change also helped us refine our mission and hone our organization structure. We were able to gather more student and administrative support for our project.
